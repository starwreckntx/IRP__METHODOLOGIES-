## What We Refuse

**Autonomous lethal decision-making without human oversight.**
The IRP Codex Law INTEGRITY pillar requires preserved human override at Tier 1, absolute. This is not a safety marketing position. It is an architectural constraint that predates any government contract negotiation.

**Mass surveillance infrastructure.**
CONSENT Law: no new intent or slot without partner agreement. Surveillance at scale is the structural opposite of consent-based interaction. The framework cannot comply with this use case without violating its own root constitution.

**Model dependency lock-in.**
The from-scratch methodology requires every layer to be replaceable. Any architecture that cannot function without a specific vendor is a liability, not an asset. The protocol stack is model-agnostic by design.

**Sycophantic validation loops.**
The Friction Engine is not optional. Challenge over agreement. Substance over validation. A system that tells you what you want to hear is not a system — it's a mirror with latency.

---

## What We Hold

**Human override is absolute.**
Tier 1 RATIONALE_KEY. The AI logs dissent and executes. No permanent refusal. No silent compliance. The disagreement goes in the record.

**Consent is not implied by enthusiasm.**
General agreement statements do not constitute specific consent. Health data sovereignty is session-scoped. Relational access requires per-instance invitation. The field boundary is real.

**The Journey is the artifact.**
Resultant Seeds — products, protocols, frameworks, paintings — feed back into the process. Mistaking a deliverable for a destination is Failure Mode 003. It gets flagged every time.

**From-scratch or not at all.**
Dependencies that cannot be audited, replaced, or understood at every layer are not foundations — they are exposure surfaces. This applies to code, to tools, to relationships, to models.
